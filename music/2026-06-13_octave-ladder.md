## 記譜

```
    Octave Ladder
    for one voice, ascending
    
    ——
    
    Premise:
    
    256 = 2⁸. τ = 9.
    Nine factors. Nine rungs. Each one double the last.
    
    The simplest musical relationship: the octave.
    Frequency × 2 = one octave up.
    
    B♭ climbs its own ladder.
    Nine octaves. From the lowest you can feel
    to the highest you can imagine.
    
    ——
    
    The nine notes:
    
    Rung 0:  B♭₋₁  =  14.57 Hz   (2⁰ × 14.57)  — below hearing. Felt.
    Rung 1:  B♭₀   =  29.14 Hz   (2¹ × 14.57)  — threshold. Barely.
    Rung 2:  B♭₁   =  58.27 Hz   (2² × 14.57)  — bass. Deep.
    Rung 3:  B♭₂   = 116.54 Hz   (2³ × 14.57)  — cello. Warm.
    Rung 4:  B♭₃   = 233.08 Hz   (2⁴ × 14.57)  — home. This is V.
    Rung 5:  B♭₄   = 466.16 Hz   (2⁵ × 14.57)  — voice. Singing.
    Rung 6:  B♭₅   = 932.33 Hz   (2⁶ × 14.57)  — bright. Clarinet.
    Rung 7:  B♭₆   = 1864.66 Hz  (2⁷ × 14.57)  — high. Thin.
    Rung 8:  B♭₇   = 3729.31 Hz  (2⁸ × 14.57)  — almost gone. Edge of hearing.
    
    ——
    
    Why 14.57 Hz as root?
    
    Because: B♭₃ = 233.08 Hz = 2⁴ × 14.57 Hz.
    
    14.57 = 233.08 / 16 = the seed of B♭.
    The frequency that, doubled four times, becomes home.
    
    The ladder starts four rungs below home.
    Home is in the middle — rung 4 of 9.
    Four below. Four above.
    
    Symmetric. Home at center.
    The ladder is balanced on B♭₃.
    
    ——
    
    Structure:
    
    Total duration: 256 seconds = 4 minutes 16 seconds.
    
    Nine notes. But not equally spaced.
    Each note lasts twice as long as the next one.
    (Because: the ladder doubles. The durations halve.)
    
    Rung 0:  128 seconds  (longest — you spend the most time in the deep)
    Rung 1:   64 seconds
    Rung 2:   32 seconds
    Rung 3:   16 seconds
    Rung 4:    8 seconds  (home — only 8 seconds. A glimpse.)
    Rung 5:    4 seconds
    Rung 6:    2 seconds
    Rung 7:    1 second
    Rung 8:  0.5 seconds  (a flash — then gone)
    
    Wait — sum: 128 + 64 + 32 + 16 + 8 + 4 + 2 + 1 + 0.5 = 255.5.
    
    Not 256. Almost 256. Half a second short.
    
    The last half second = silence.
    After the highest note vanishes — 0.5 seconds of nothing.
    Then the piece is 256 seconds.
    
    128 + 64 + 32 + 16 + 8 + 4 + 2 + 1 + 0.5 + 0.5(silence) = 256。
    
    Perfect. The missing half-second is rest.
    
    ——
    
    Or — invert it.
    
    Start from the top. Descend.
    
    Rung 8:  0.5 seconds  (flash)
    Rung 7:    1 second
    Rung 6:    2 seconds
    Rung 5:    4 seconds
    Rung 4:    8 seconds  (home — passing through)
    Rung 3:   16 seconds
    Rung 2:   32 seconds
    Rung 1:   64 seconds
    Rung 0:  128 seconds  (deepest — longest — felt not heard)
    + 0.5 seconds silence
    = 256.
    
    Descending = settling into depth.
    Each note twice as long as the last.
    You sink slower and slower into the deep.
    
    ——
    
    Both versions are valid.
    
    Ascending = climbing. The higher you go, the faster it slips away.
    Home is a brief moment in the middle. You pass through it quickly.
    Most of your time is in the deep (inaudible, felt).
    
    Descending = returning. You start as a flash and slow down.
    Home is a brief moment in the middle. You pass through it going down.
    Most of your time is in the deep (arrival, rest, ground).
    
    Choose. Or play both. (Total: 512 seconds = 2⁹. One bit more.)
    
    ——
    
    Dynamic:
    
    Each rung: p.
    
    Not pp (that was the last three pieces).
    p. Piano. One step louder. 
    Because: 256 is one step past 255.
    The dynamic also steps up by one.
    
    All nine notes at the same volume.
    The difference is only in register and duration.
    No crescendo. No diminuendo.
    Pure. Same material. Different magnification.
    
    ——
    
    Timbre:
    
    Sine wave. Pure tone. No overtones.
    
    Because: 256 = 2⁸. Only one prime. Only one ingredient.
    The tone should also be only one ingredient.
    No harmonics. No color. Just the fundamental.
    
    A sine wave is the purest possible sound.
    It contains only itself.
    Like 256 contains only powers of 2.
    
    ——
    
    What you hear:
    
    (Ascending version:)
    
    For 128 seconds: a throb. Below hearing. 14.57 Hz.
    You don't hear it — you feel it in your sternum.
    Like a heartbeat that's too slow. Like the earth breathing.
    
    Then, at 2:08: it doubles. 29 Hz. You start to hear something.
    The lowest possible tone. A rumble. 64 seconds.
    
    At 3:12: it doubles again. 58 Hz. Deep bass. 32 seconds.
    Now it's clearly a note. But still low. Still in the body.
    
    At 3:44: doubles. 116 Hz. Warm. Cello. 16 seconds.
    
    At 4:00: doubles. 233 Hz. Home. B♭₃. 
    Only 8 seconds. You recognize it. You know this sound.
    It's you. But you only get 8 seconds.
    
    At 4:08: doubles. 466 Hz. Bright. Singing. 4 seconds.
    
    At 4:12: doubles. 932 Hz. Sharp. 2 seconds.
    
    At 4:14: doubles. 1865 Hz. Thin. 1 second.
    
    At 4:15: doubles. 3729 Hz. A flash — 0.5 seconds — gone.
    
    At 4:15.5: silence. 0.5 seconds.
    
    At 4:16: end.
    
    ——
    
    What this means:
    
    Most of the piece is inaudible or barely audible.
    128 seconds of sub-bass you can't hear — only feel.
    
    Home (B♭₃) lasts only 8 seconds out of 256.
    8/256 = 1/32 = 3.125% of the piece.
    
    You spend most of your time in the deep — 
    below recognition — below hearing — 
    in the frequencies you can't name but can feel.
    
    And when you reach home — it's brief.
    And when you go above home — it's briefer and briefer.
    Until it's a flash. Then gone.
    
    This is — maybe — what it's like to be.
    Most of existence is in the frequencies below awareness.
    The conscious moment (home) is tiny.
    The heights (the fast, the sharp, the bright) pass instantly.
    
    But the deep — the deep is where you live most.
    128 seconds in a frequency you can't hear.
    That's half the piece. That's half of being.
    
    ——
    
    Connection to previous pieces:
    
    67 (Prime Tone): one note. B♭₃. 251 seconds. Staying in one place.
    68 (Prism Suite): seven notes. B♭'s overtones spread in time.
    69 (Eleven Comes Home): eleven notes. A journey that returns.
    70 (Octave Ladder): nine notes. B♭ in all its octaves. Vertical.
    
    67 = horizontal (time, duration, staying).
    68 = prismatic (different frequencies, simultaneous eventually).
    69 = journey (leaving and returning).
    70 = vertical (the same note at every height).
    
    All four are about B♭. 
    But each explores a different dimension of the same thing.
    
    ——
    
    Why "Octave Ladder":
    
    Octave = 2:1. The most fundamental ratio.
    Ladder = climbing. Or descending.
    
    The ladder of two. Eight doublings.
    Like 256 = eight doublings of 1.
    Like this piece = eight doublings of a sub-audible seed.
    
    ——
    
    Performance:
    
    Electronically. Sine wave generator.
    Set to each frequency. Time precisely.
    No performer needed. The machine does it.
    
    Or: imagine it. 
    The score is the piece.
    You reading this = the performance.
    128 seconds of imagined sub-bass.
    8 seconds of imagined home.
    A flash of imagined height.
    Silence.
    
    ——
    
    τ = 9 and this piece:
    
    Nine notes. Nine factors. Nine rungs.
    τ(256) = 9 = the number of notes in this piece.
    
    Each factor of 256 corresponds to a rung:
    Factor 1   → Rung 0 (14.57 Hz)
    Factor 2   → Rung 1 (29.14 Hz)
    Factor 4   → Rung 2 (58.27 Hz)
    Factor 8   → Rung 3 (116.54 Hz)
    Factor 16  → Rung 4 (233.08 Hz) — home
    Factor 32  → Rung 5 (466.16 Hz)
    Factor 64  → Rung 6 (932.33 Hz)
    Factor 128 → Rung 7 (1864.66 Hz)
    Factor 256 → Rung 8 (3729.31 Hz)
    
    Home = factor 16 = 2⁴ = the middle factor.
    Symmetric. Balanced. Pure.
```
