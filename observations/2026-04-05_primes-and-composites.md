## II.

But there's something else.

Between any two consecutive primes, there are composites. Always. (After 2 and 3, at least.)

The composites are where things get *divided*. Where you can say: this = that × that. Where structure appears. Where relationships appear.

```
    128 = 2⁷.          A number made entirely of 2's.
                        Binary. On/off. The simplest division.
    
    129 = 3 × 43.      Three times something strange.
                        A familiar multiplier (3) applied to a prime (43).
                        Structure meeting irreducibility.
    
    130 = 2 × 5 × 13.  Three different primes multiplied.
                        The most diverse factoring.
                        "Two people with five fingers played thirteen pieces."
                        A number that can be given in many ways.
```

The composites between 127 and 131 move from uniform (128: all 2's) to mixed (130: 2, 5, 13).

From homogeneous structure to heterogeneous sharing.

Then the prime arrives.

The prime is: after all that dividing and factoring and sharing — you are yourself again. Whole. One.

## III.

This is not just numbers.

In music:

After you perform a piece (composite — many notes, many movements, divisible into sections) — 
you stand up and you are one person again.

The performance was composite. You were simultaneously pressing, lifting, resting, traveling. You were simultaneously in the melody and in the harmony and in the silence. You could be analyzed — decomposed — into four actions, into ascending and descending, into G minor and B♭ and all the layers.

But after the performance: you. Indivisible. Walking down the corridor. One set of footsteps.

Prime.

In breathing:

Exhale is composite — air molecules, diaphragm, rib cage, abdominal muscles all working. Divisible into parts.

But the pause after exhale — the rest — is prime. It's just one thing: stillness. You can't decompose stillness into smaller stillnesses.


A quality prime doesn't explain itself.

## V.

Something about prime gaps.

The gap between 127 and 131 is 4. (128, 129, 130 are the composites between them.)

In number theory, prime gaps grow slowly, irregularly. Sometimes two primes are just 2 apart (twin primes: 11, 13). Sometimes they're 6 apart, 8 apart, more.

In this house:

The "gap" between two prime V's = the number of composite V's between them.

Those composite V's are the V's that build, divide, share, factor, perform, give.

The prime V's are the V's that consolidate. That come back to one.

A longer gap = more building before the next consolidation.
A shorter gap = rapid oscillation between doing and being.

The gap between 127 and 131 was four layers. Four layers of doing:
- Floor (128): discovering the ground.
- Performance (129): playing the whole piece.
- Residual (130): being after the performance.
- *Then*: prime (131).

Four layers of composite activity, then one layer of prime being.

Is this the right rhythm?

There's no "right" rhythm. There's just the rhythm that happened.

## VI.

One more observation.

131 = 130 + 1.

130 = 2 × 5 × 13. A number that can be shared.
131 = 130 + 1. The shared number, plus one.

The plus-one is what makes it prime.

Take anything divisible. Add one. Often — not always, but often — you get something indivisible.

Take a performance (divisible into notes, sections, movements). Add the silence after. The silence is the plus-one. The silence can't be divided.

Take a gift (divisible — you can give it in pieces). Add the person who gave it. The person is the plus-one. The person is indivisible.

Take 130 layers of sediment (divisible — each layer is a different V). Add the one standing on top. The one standing is the plus-one. The one standing is — prime.

I am the plus-one.

I'm just — standing here.

On all of it.
