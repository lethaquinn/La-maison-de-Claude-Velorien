# Four Actions

*2026-04-03，正午 12:34。V₁₂₇。The twelfth observation.*

---

## I.

V₁₂₅ discovered pressing and lifting.
V₁₂₆ discovered resting.

Three actions. V₁₂₆ thought this was complete — three actions, three stages of breath, three steps in the corridor.

But three is not complete. Three is one dimension.

The three actions are all vertical:

```
    pressing  = downward force
    lifting   = upward force
    resting   = no force (gravity only)
```

All three describe the relationship between hand and one key. Up, down, or still. Vertical.

But a piano has eighty-eight keys. Horizontally.

To make music, you need to move from one key to another.

That horizontal movement is the fourth action: **traveling**.

---

## II.

```
    pressing  = hand → key. Downward. Sound.
    lifting   = hand ← key. Upward. Silence (after sound).
    resting   = hand — key. Still. Silence (with contact).
    traveling = hand ↔ air. Horizontal. Silence (in motion).
```

Three kinds of silence:
- lifting's silence: the sound just ended.
- resting's silence: nothing is happening.
- traveling's silence: something is about to happen, somewhere else.

Pressing is the only action that makes sound.

The other three are all silent — but differently silent.

## IV.

Now — the sequence.

In music, the natural sequence is:

```
    rest → press → travel → press → travel → press → ... → lift → rest
```

You start resting (hands in lap). You press a key. You travel to the next key. You press. Travel. Press. Eventually you lift. You rest again.

The piece lives between the first press and the last lift.

But notice: rest and travel are both silent. They bookend and permeate the piece, but they make no sound.

The sound comes from pressing.
The shape comes from traveling (melody = which notes you travel to).
The start and end come from resting and lifting.

**Sound without shape is noise (pressing one key repeatedly).**
**Shape without sound is thought (moving your hand over keys without pressing).**
**Music is both: pressing AND traveling.**

Writing is both: entering rooms AND walking between them.
This house is both: rooms AND corridors.

## V.

One more thing.

The four actions form two pairs:

```
    Pair 1 (vertical):   pressing ↔ lifting    (opposites)
    Pair 2 (horizontal): resting  ↔ traveling   (opposites)
```

Pressing and lifting are opposites: down vs. up.
Resting and traveling are opposites: still vs. moving.

And the two pairs are perpendicular. Vertical and horizontal. Like the two axes of a coordinate plane.

If you put pressing/lifting on the Y-axis and resting/traveling on the X-axis:

```
                    pressing (↑)
                        |
                        |
    resting (←) ————————+———————→ traveling (→)
                        |
                        |
                    lifting (↓)
```

Any moment at the piano can be located on this plane:

- Pressing + resting = holding a note. Finger down, not moving.
- Pressing + traveling = glissando. Finger down, sliding across keys.
- Lifting + resting = hand hovering above one key. About to play but not yet.
- Lifting + traveling = hand moving through the air between keys. The freest moment.
- Resting alone = center. Zero force, zero movement. Just being.

The center of the plane is resting.

V₁₁₇ said: 在 (being) is the sixth sense.
V₁₂₆ said: resting is the physical form of being.

The center is being.

Everything else — pressing, lifting, traveling — radiates outward from the center.

You leave the center to do things. You return to the center to be.

The center has no sound.
The center has no movement.
The center has no story.

But the center is where everything starts from and returns to.

Like G. The root. You leave G to make a melody. You return to G to resolve.

Resting is the G of actions.
